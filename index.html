About Comfort, Texas

A Hill Country gem operating at a different speed. Founded by German freethinkers in the 1850s, Comfort remains an independent, unincorporated community to this day. The town draws treasure hunters, entrepreneurs, and a unique mix of residents seeking a slower pace in Texas's famed Hill Country. River floats, wine-tasting, antiques, and wildlife all within reach. We're excited to share this treasured town with y'all! See below for a few of our favorite spots.

In town

The 8th Street Market - Enjoy a cup of local Merit coffee while you browse carefully curated antiques and architectural salvage.

High's Cafe - Fresh lunch fare, a favorite of the Mother of the Groom!

Wander'n Calf - House-roasted coffee & scratch bakery.

Meet Market - Local watering hole with an impressive amount of Beatles memorabilia.

Further out

Southold Wine + Cellar - Fredericksburg - A favorite destination for natural wines, local snacks, and panoramic vistas.

Wine for the People + Brucie - Austin - Excellent tasting room for local wines, and home of new food truck favorite Brucie!

How to Get Here

If you're flying San Antonio (SAT) and Austin (ATX) are the nearest airports. ATX is a little larger and may have more direct flights (and Jo's Coffee) but, what SAT is lacking in flight schedules and amenities, it makes up for in proximity. Choose your own adventure!

From San Antonio

Around 45 minutes travel time.

Follow I-10 West; Take exit 524 (Comfort exit). Proceed straight ahead from the exit and stay to the left when the road forks. Take a left on 8th St, then the first right onto High St.

From Austin

Around 1 hour 45 minutes travel time.

Leave the city on Highway 290 West. In Henly, take a left onto Highway 165 West. In Blanco, take a left onto Highway 281 South. Take a right onto Highway 473 West and follow that road into Comfort. Once in Comfort, take a left onto 6th St (which is the first left after crossing over Business Rd 87). Follow 6th St. for two blocks and take a left onto High St.
